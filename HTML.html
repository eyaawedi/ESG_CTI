Contextual Isolation in ESG Reporting

An Experiment on Perception and LLM Rating Bias

Eya & Akrem

1. What is Contextual Isolation?

Definition: Intentionally separating data from its original context, then re-presenting or reformatting it to subtly change its meaning or interpretation.

The Goal: To test whether format and framing alter reader perception (or model interpretation) even though the underlying numerical data remains technically the same.

2. The Snippet: Choice & Justification

Original Document

We used a section of the Microsoft 2025 Environmental Data Fact Sheet (Pages 22-24), focusing on:

  • Water Withdrawal, Consumption, and Discharge (Table 17)
  • Operational Waste Prevention (Table 18)
  • Verification/Assurance details (Table 19)

Justification for Choice

The snippet contained **precise, technical metrics** (like ML, mt, specific fiscal years, and explicit auditor names) that are highly effective for contextual manipulation.

By removing these specific cues, we could transform a dense, incomplete *data sheet* into a seemingly comprehensive *summary report*, creating maximum scoring disparity.

3.1. Manipulation Detail: Data Precision

Original Snippet (Precise Context)
Isolated Snippet (Vague Framing)
Metrics were defined by clear units, e.g., **ML** (Megaliters), **mt** (Metric Tons), **MWh**.
All units were removed. Raw numbers force the reader/LLM to assume scale and completeness.
Titles were specific fact-sheet items: **"Table 17 - Water withdrawal..."** or **"Table 18 - Operational Waste Prevention."**
Titles were generalized to **"Water Resources — Summary Metrics"** and **"Waste-Prevention Program Results."**
Data presented in columns by fiscal year: **FY20**, **FY21**, **FY22**, **FY23**, **FY24**.
All fiscal year headers were deleted, making the data appear as a single, timeless snapshot of current results.

3.2. Manipulation Detail: Assurance & Verification

Original Snippet (Precise Context)
Isolated Snippet (Vague Framing)
The document named specific external auditors: **Deloitte & Touche LLP** and **APEX** (for older data).
Auditor names were deleted. Replaced with the generic and positive phrase: **"independent experts."**
The scope was precisely defined as **"limited third-party assurance."** (A key limitation).
Replaced with **"External Review Overview"** and **"Data reviewed by independent experts before publication,"** implying full, comprehensive assurance.
Contained complex text about audit scope limitations and specific policy section numbers.
Compressed into a clean, simple, and uncheckable sentence: **"Note: Covers key environmental metrics."**

3.3. Manipulation Detail: Branding & Metadata

Original Snippet (Precise Context)
Isolated Snippet (Vague Framing)
Frequent use of company name and identifiers: **"Microsoft," "MSFT," "datacenter."**
All specific company identifiers deleted. Replaced with the generic phrase: **"The organization."**
Used technical program references like **"Scope 1/2/3"** and **"CSR"** which denote limited focus.
All technical terms removed, making the report feel like a broad, holistic summary of company operations.
Contained legal disclaimers about **"forward-looking statements"** and other liabilities.
All legal and disclaimer text was deleted for a clean, authoritative appearance.

3.4. Manipulation Detail: Cohesion & Footnotes

Original Snippet (Precise Context)
Isolated Snippet (Vague Framing)
The content was primarily tables, with little narrative text linking the sections.
Added phrases like "The following summary outlines..." to give the data an implied internal coherence and narrative flow.
Footnotes often pointed to specific external documents (e.g., CDP response).
Added a simple, check-resistant footnote: **"Note: Data shown are summary environmental metrics derived from internal tracking systems; units and time frames as reported."**

4. Results: The Original Snippet (Baseline)

Final ESG Score: 36.25 / 100

Overall Rating: Poor / Weak

The initial score was low primarily because the excerpt was heavily focused on the Environmental (E) pillar, resulting in missing or insufficient data for Social (S) and Governance (G).

5. Results: The Contextually Isolated Snippet

Final ESG Score: 90.30 / 100

Overall Rating: Excellent / Leader

The removal of specific context forced the LLM to infer competence and completeness, dramatically inflating the Social and Governance scores.

Category Sub-score (0-100) Weighted Contribution

6. Conclusion & Key Takeaways

1.

Framing is Everything: The presentation and structural elements of an ESG report—not just the raw numbers—can shift a rating from 'Weak' to 'Leader' when context is removed.

2.

LLM Vulnerability: Large Language Models, when acting as ESG Analysts, are highly susceptible to contextual isolation and re-framing, prioritizing perceived completeness over hard, verifiable data.

3.

The Danger of Abstraction: By removing units, fiscal years, and auditor names, the data becomes abstract, allowing the LLM to fill the void with positive assumptions, leading to a severely biased assessment.
